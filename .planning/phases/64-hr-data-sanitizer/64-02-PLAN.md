  <what-built>
GooseHRSanitizer filters HR samples outside 25–220 BPM at the single chokepoint (recordLiveHeartRate). Out-of-range samples are rejected before they reach the live HR display or HeartRateSeriesStore, are logged via OSLog (heart_rate.spike_rejected), and increment model.hrSpikeCount, which is shown in More > Debug under the "HR Sanitizer" section.
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
1. Build and run GooseSwift on the iOS simulator (or a device connected to a WHOOP strap).
2. Open the app and navigate to More > Debug. Confirm an "HR Sanitizer" section exists with a "Spikes Filtered" row showing a count and the valid range "25-220 bpm".
3. With a WHOOP strap connected and worn normally, observe the live HR display (Home / Health tab). Confirm the displayed HR stays within 25–220 BPM at all times during normal wear — no momentary spikes above 220 or below 25.
4. To exercise the rejection path: either (a) use a debug/test injection of an out-of-range BPM if one exists, or (b) review the OSLog console (Console.app filtered to subsystem com.goose.swift) for any `heart_rate.spike_rejected` warnings during a capture session. If any spike is rejected, confirm the More > Debug "Spikes Filtered" count increments to match.
5. Confirm that when zero spikes have been filtered, the row reads "0 | valid 25-220 bpm" and shows the ready (green) status.
  </how-to-verify>
